How to Check Your WPM Accurately

Checking your WPM takes under a minute, but getting a number you can actually trust takes a little more care. Here is what a WPM checker really measures, how to test your typing speed so the result reflects reality, and how to turn one lucky run into a dependable figure.

What "WPM" means before you check it

WPM stands for words per minute. Because real words vary in length, typing tests don't count actual words — they use a standardized "word" of five characters, spaces included. So when a WPM checker reports your speed, it is really counting your correct characters, dividing by five, and scaling that to a full minute:

WPM = (correct characters ÷ 5) ÷ minutes elapsed

The key phrase is correct characters. A trustworthy checker reports net WPM — speed measured only on the keystrokes you got right — rather than flattering raw speed that ignores your mistakes. That's the number worth knowing, because it reflects how much usable text you can actually produce.

How to check your WPM, step by step

Using an online WPM checker is simple, but a few small choices make the difference between a noisy score and a reliable one:

  1. Pick a duration. A 60-second test gives the most stable reading; shorter 15 or 30-second runs are quicker but bounce around more. If a job screening or class uses one minute, match that.
  2. Read the passage, then type it. Type the text exactly as shown. Don't skim ahead so far that you lose your place — smooth, steady typing beats bursts.
  3. Keep your eyes on the screen. Glancing at the keyboard slows you down and hides errors until it's too late to feel them in your rhythm.
  4. Let it run to the end. Stopping early or restarting the moment you make a mistake trains you to panic, not to type. Finish the run.
  5. Read both numbers. Note your WPM and your accuracy. One without the other tells you very little.

How to get an accurate result, not just a fast one

The measurement in a good checker is precise to the millisecond. What varies is you, from one run to the next. To make your score reflect your true speed rather than a fluke:

  • Warm up first. Your very first run of the day is almost always your slowest. Take two or three throwaway attempts before you record anything.
  • Test on your normal keyboard. A laptop, a mechanical board, and a phone all produce different numbers. Use whatever you type on day to day.
  • Sit the way you normally sit. Posture, wrist angle, and desk height all feed into your speed. Recreate your real conditions.
  • Use fresh text. If you've memorized a passage, you're testing recall, not typing. A good checker serves new text each run so you can't game it.
  • Average several runs. Take the same length test three to five times and average the results. A single short run has real natural variation; the average smooths it out.

Do those five things and the number you get will closely track your genuine, everyday typing speed — the one that actually matters for work, study, or a screening.

Why accuracy belongs in every WPM check

It's tempting to chase a big WPM figure and ignore accuracy, but the two are joined at the hip. Accuracy is the share of your keystrokes that matched the target text:

Accuracy = (correct characters ÷ total characters typed) × 100%

A run at 85 WPM with 88 percent accuracy is worse in practice than a run at 70 WPM with 98 percent accuracy, because every mistake in the first run costs a backspace, a retype, and a broken rhythm. That's why a WPM checker that counts only correct characters gives you a more honest picture than one that rewards fast, sloppy typing. What's a good number once you've checked?

Once you have a reliable WPM, here's rough context. The average adult types around 40 WPM. Anything above 60 WPM is generally considered good, 80 WPM or more is fast, and professional typists often exceed 100 WPM. Frequently asked questions

How do I check my WPM?
Use an online WPM checker: pick a time limit, type the passage shown as accurately as you can, and read your words-per-minute score at the end. For a reliable number, run a 60-second test three to five times and average the results.
What is a WPM checker actually measuring?
A good WPM checker measures net speed — it counts only the characters you typed correctly, divides by five to convert characters into standardized words, and scales to a full minute. It also tracks accuracy, the share of keystrokes that matched the target text.
How can I check my typing speed accurately?
Test on your normal keyboard, warm up first, sit in your usual posture, and use text you haven't memorized. Take several runs and average them. Watch accuracy alongside speed, because a fast run full of errors overstates your real usable WPM.
Is an online WPM checker accurate?
The measurement itself is precise, since the test records keystrokes with millisecond timing. The variable is you: fatigue, unfamiliar text, and a strange keyboard all shift the result. Averaging several runs on your own setup gives a number close to your true speed.
