Average Typing Speed by Age

How does your typing speed compare to other people your age? While individual ability varies enormously, decades of typing research point to fairly consistent patterns across age groups. Here are the typical benchmarks — and why age matters less than you might think.

Typical WPM by age group

These figures are general averages for people typing in their native language on a standard keyboard. Treat them as rough reference points, not strict rules.

Age groupTypical typing speed
Children (6–11)15–25 WPM
Teens (12–17)30–45 WPM
Young adults (18–25)40–55 WPM
Adults (26–50)40–60 WPM
Older adults (50+)35–55 WPM

Why children type more slowly

Younger children are still developing fine motor control, letter recognition, and reading speed — all of which feed into typing. A common rule of thumb for students is roughly five WPM per grade level, so a 4th grader around 20 WPM and an 8th grader around 40 WPM would both be on track. Early, correct touch typing instruction tends to pay off for years.

The teen and young-adult peak

Teenagers and young adults often post the fastest raw speeds. They combine quick reflexes with heavy daily keyboard use for school, messaging, and gaming. But raw reflexes are only part of the story — technique and practice matter far more, which is why a motivated adult can easily out-type a faster-reflexed teenager.

Does typing slow down with age?

Surprisingly little, for skilled typists. Research on expert typists shows that older typists with good technique often match younger ones, even when their isolated reaction times are slower. They compensate by reading further ahead and anticipating upcoming keys. In other words, experience and technique offset the small slowdown in pure reflex speed.

What actually drives your speed

Age is a weak predictor of typing speed. The factors that really move the needle are:

  • Technique — touch typing versus hunt-and-peck is the single biggest difference.
  • Practice frequency — daily keyboard use builds and maintains muscle memory.
  • Accuracy — fewer mistakes mean fewer corrections and a higher net speed.
  • Familiarity with the text — common words flow faster than unfamiliar terms.

Frequently asked questions

What is the average typing speed for an adult?
Around 40 words per minute. Regular computer users often reach 50 to 60 WPM, and trained typists commonly exceed 80 WPM.
Does typing speed decline with age?
Not significantly for skilled typists. Older typists may have slightly slower reaction times but compensate with better technique, often matching younger typists.
How fast should a child be able to type?
A reasonable target is about five WPM per grade level — roughly 25 WPM for a 5th grader and 40 to 50 WPM for a practiced high schooler.
