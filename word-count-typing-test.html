What is a typing test by word count?

Instead of stopping after a fixed number of seconds, this test stops after a fixed number of words. You choose the target — 10, 25, 50, or 100 — and the passage is generated to match that exact count. The test ends the instant you finish typing it, and your WPM is calculated from however long that actually took you.

How to use it

  • Pick a word count. Choose 10, 25, 50, or 100 words from the selector above.
  • Start typing. Click the passage or start typing to begin. The clock starts on your first keystroke.
  • Finish the passage. The test ends automatically once you've typed the full word count — there's no timer to watch.
  • Track your progress. The words-typed counter shows how far through the passage you are in real time.

Word count vs. timed tests

A timed test (like our standard 15/30/60-second typing test) is great for a quick pulse check, but the amount of text you type varies with your speed. A word-count test flips that: the amount of text is fixed, so it's a more consistent way to compare two attempts, or to compare your speed against someone else who typed the exact same length of text.

Which word count should you pick?

10 or 25 words is fast — good for a quick warm-up or a repeatable short benchmark. 50 words is a solid everyday default that balances speed and stability. 100 words takes longer but smooths out the effect of a slow start, giving you the most stable WPM reading. If you want a change of pace, the quote typing test and code typing test use the same WPM math with different source material.

Frequently asked questions

How is a typing test by word count different from a timed test?
A timed test ends after a fixed number of seconds no matter how much you've typed. A word-count test ends after you finish typing a fixed number of words, no matter how long it takes, so your result reflects a consistent amount of text every time.
Which word count should I choose?
10 or 25 words is a quick check of your current speed. 50 words is a good default for regular practice. 100 words gives the most stable, repeatable WPM reading because early hesitation matters less over a longer passage.
Is WPM calculated the same way as the timed typing test?
Yes. WPM is still correctly typed characters divided by five, scaled to a full minute, based on the actual time it took you to finish the selected word count. Accuracy is calculated the same way too.
